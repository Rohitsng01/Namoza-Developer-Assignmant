Non-Surgical Management of Knee Osteoarthritis

Published: July 2026 | By: Dr. Amitav Sen | Read Time: 5 mins

1. Understanding Cartilage Wear: Osteoarthritis (OA) is the wear-and-tear of articular cartilage. While joint replacement surgery is highly successful for advanced-stage OA, early to moderate knee pain can be effectively managed without operating. The objective is to stabilize the joint, reduce inflammatory flares, and maintain joint lubrication.

2. Core Strengthening & Physiotherapy: The knee joint is heavily dependent on surrounding muscular stability. Strengthening the quadriceps, hamstrings, and gluteal muscle groups transfers weight bearing away from the worn cartilage. Daily low-impact exercises, such as stationary bicycling and straight leg raises, increase regional blood flow and joint mobility.

3. Weight Management Dynamics: Physics plays a crucial role in knee health. Every kilogram of body weight translates to four times the force acting on the knee joint during walking. Losing even 5% of body weight can reduce joint strain by up to 20%, significantly mitigating pain signals during daily activities like climbing stairs.

4. Advanced Medical Therapies: For persistent discomfort, orthopaedic clinics offer knee lubrication injections (Hyaluronic acid) and regenerative therapies (PRP) which act as shock absorbers. Viscosupplementation provides mechanical cushion inside the joint space, offering pain relief that ranges from 6 to 12 months without systemic side effects.

5. Structured Guidance: Always consult an experienced orthopaedic specialist to draft a personalized rehabilitation schedule. Knowing when to utilize hot compresses versus ice wraps is crucial in preventing chronic ligamentous stiffness.
